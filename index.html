About Me

My name is Josh Aney, and I am currently a computer science student at Montana State University. I am in my third year and I plan on graduating in the spring of 2026. Ever since I have been young, I have always been fascinated with how computers, video games, or any other sort of software worked. I could tell from a young age that I would be interested in pushing the limits of technology and applying it into my own day-to-day life to solve problems

When I am not completing school work or working on other projects, I enjoy spending time outdoors. I love getting out into the mountains to ski or bike. I live for any sort of adventures that will get me outside

Academic Interests

In school, I have grown to be very interested in the world of machine learning. I enjoy working with a group of my peers to solve a problem that is given to us. Working in a group gives me a chance to bounce ideas off of other people to allow me to find new ways to manipulate a dataset or approach a certain challenge. I also love getting to work with the math involved in many of these problems. I had the opportunity to code a few common machine learning algorithms from scratch, and it truly gave me a new perspective of why math is so important in my everyday life.

Professional Experience

I am currently working with the CaMP (Cybernetics and Motor Physiology) lab at Mayo Clinic. I worked with Dr. Kai Miller on a research project that involved me analyzing voltage traces from the brain with different machine learning techniques. This experience taught me a lot about how to handle real world challenges that could be faced when working in the machine learning field. I often had to work with small datasets that would have missing values or noisy data. This caused me to be very conscious on how I trained the models and how I chose which models to use.

Extracurricular Activities

For the past two years, I have been able to be a part of the Spirit of the West Drumline. I started playing musical instruments at a young age, and I have been hooked ever since. I now have the privilege of playing snare drum at all of Montana State University's home football games which has been one of the best experiences of my entire life.

I am also Co-President of the Montana State Club Tennis Team. Sports have always been a large part of my life since a young age, and I am so happy that I am able to give students an opportunity to continue playing or pick up tennis at a low cost when they come to college.
